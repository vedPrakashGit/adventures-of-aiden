The Bull and the Bridge

Witches, dragons, goblins, and magic! What child can resist short storieslike this, especially when paired with special bonding time with a parent? The short stories that make up theAdventures of Aiden ​series take parents back to the days of their own favorite fairy tales and fables, and children are being introduced to magical stories for kids with life lessons hidden within. It’s a winning proposition for both parents and kids alike

Central to the stories for kidsin The Adventures of Aiden ​are messages of anti-bullying, a serious problem for children of all ages. “The Bull and the Bridge,” one of these tales, uses the story of a girl’s search for her own unique strengths to teach children to foster confidence.


The Bull and the Bridge

In this story, Skylar and her brother, Aiden, are going on a safari with their dad. They are looking forward to seeing lots of big game animals, like elephants and giraffes. But when Dad compliments Aiden on his great baseball game the previous day, Skylar is wondering how she can feel special too. Her dad tucks her in bed their first night in the hotel and tells her the story of Princess Skylar, who is led on a journey with her fairy friend, Malia.


Princess Skylar is soon faced with a decision: Does she cross the rickety bridge that leads her to uncover her specialness, even though she has to face a scary bull, or does she turn around and go home? She decides to face her fear, and on the other side of the bridge she finds a magic fountain that gives her a glimpse at all the incredible traits she has inside: courage, joy, love, and creativity.


“The Bull and the Bridge” weaves a children’s bedtime story into the larger picture of building a child’s strength from the inside out. This is the kind of character-building that creates lasting, positive change in children. And it relates to anti-bullying efforts, too.


The Connection Between Children’s Stories and Bullying

A child that feels inner strength and confidence is less likely to be affected by bullying. Children’s literaturelike The Adventures of Aiden ​tells stories of characters overcoming their own problems with the help of magical creatures or caring adults. Lessons like this can create lasting change.


Short stories for kidsthat include lessons like these can help children learn to:


  • Handle everyday problems.
  • Build emotional resilience.
  • Learn about other cultures.
  • Develop imagination.
  • Learn critical thinking skills.
  • Pass along cultural mores.

Parents and teachers may think reading fairy tales is an activity without much substance, but there is so much more than that going on. Children who know how to solve problems using critical thinking skills and imagination instead of avoiding issues out of fear are building a core of internal strength. They can then call on that strength when faced with a bully who attempts to belittle and harass them.


Conclusion

The Adventures of Aiden,​with stories for kidslike “The Bull and the Bridge,” can complement what parents and teachers do at home and in school to combat bullying. Reading and discussing anti-bullying fairy tales and other forms of children’s literature with kids can have a huge impact on building inner strength. When faced with a bully, children then have that strength to call on.
